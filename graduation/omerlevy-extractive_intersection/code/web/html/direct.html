Sentence Fusion


Task Definition
You are given two sentences that have some overlap in meaning.
Your task is to generate their intersection: a new sentence that contains all the information these sentences have in common, and only that information.
You may only use words that appeared in the original sentences. For example:

   Sentence 1:     President Obama visited Canada on Wednesday.  
   Sentence 2:     The president flew to Ottawa for a meeting with Trudeau.  
   Intersections:   ✔ The president visited Canada. This is the gold-standard intersection  
    ✔ Obama visited Canada. General knowledge implies that "The president" refers to "Obama", so this one is also fine  
    ✘ The president visited [somewhere]. Canada is implied by both sentences, and should appear in the intersection  
    ✘ The president visited Canada on Wednesday. The time is only mentioned in one sentence, and should not appear in the intersection  
    ✘ Barack Obama visited Canada. The word "Barack" did not appear in the original sentences  

Constructing New Sentences
Construct the new sentence by selecting (clicking on) words from the original sentences in order. Words from the new sentence can be removed by clicking on them.
Placeholders can be used when an appropriate word is not available. For example, if "John went to school" and "Mary went to school", then "[someone] went to school".
Be flexible, and allow for ungrammatical inflections, such as "go"/"goes", "a"/"an", "book"/"books", etc.

Finishing Up
Once you have finished constructing a sentence, click on the "Check-in" button to confirm. This can be undone by clicking "Undo".
There may be multiple valid intersection sentences, e.g. "The president visited Canada" / "Obama visited Canada". If possible, try to create a few different intersection sentences, where all of them have the same meaning.
When you have finished creating all the possible intersections, click "Submit".

Above all, use your common sense. Good luck!
