Entailment


These questions were generated from your alignment. Answer them with common sense.

Each question consists of a premise (the first sentence) and a hypothesis (the second sentence).
Assuming that the premise is true, you need to decide whether the hypothesis is true as well. For example:

   Premise:     President Obama flew to Ottawa on Wednesday.  
   Hypotheses:   ✔ The president visited Canada. It is common knowledge that Ottawa is in Canada  
    ✔ Obama went [somewhere] on Wednesday. Placeholders, such as [somewhere], can be used to generalize  
    ✘ Obama went [somewhere] on Tuesday. The events of Tuesday cannot be inferred from the premise  
    ✘ Obama ate Canadian bacon. What Obama did or did not eat cannot be inferred from the premise  

Some questions contain dropdown menus with placeholders, such as [something].
Select the best fit that still keeps the hypothesis valid. If none of the placeholders makes the hypothesis valid, select "No".

In any case, if the hypothesis is ungrammatical or does not make sense, select "It's not a sentence".

Note: certain combinations of answers may automatically disable (gray out) other questions. That's fine.
